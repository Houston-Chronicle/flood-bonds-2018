Harris County's $2.5 billion flood bond:

Search the projects in your neighborhood

By Jordan Rubio, Rachael Gleason and Zach Despart

Harris County voters approved the county flood bond on Saturday, Aug. 25. In addition to the $2.5 billion in the flood bond, the county will also have access to as much as $2 billion in federal matching funds. To read more about how the flood bond affects you, click here.

Explore the bond infrastructure projects in your neighborhood by searching for your ZIP code or watershed below.

Total buyout projects: {{zip.buyout}}

Buying flood-prone properties from willing sellers in areas that are located too deep in the floodplain for flood risk reduction projects to help out.

Total community input projects: {{zip.input}}

These projects are suggested by community members, sometimes during community engagement meetings.

Total local projects: {{zip.local}}

These are projects that are solely funded through the Harris County Flood Control District.

Total partnership projects: {{zip.partnership}}

Projects funded using a combination of Harris County Flood Control District funding and funds from other local, state, or federal sources.

Total storm repair projects: {{zip.stormRepair}}

These projects repair storm channels and stormwater detention basins.

Total subdivision drainage improvement projects: {{zip.subdivision}}

These projects, done in partnership with a Municipal Utility District and the Harris County Engineering Departnment, improve drainage in subdivisions in unincorporated parts of Harris County.

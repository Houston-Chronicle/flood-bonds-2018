Harris County's $2.5 billion flood bond:

Get insights into the bonds here


Breaking down the projects

The 237 flood infrastructure projects include $1.2 billion for channel improvements, $401 million for detention basins, $242 million for floodplain land acquisition, $12.5 million for new floodplain mapping and $1.25 million for an improved early flood warning system.

Source: Harris County Flood Control District | Data analysis by Jordan Rubio | Map created by Rachael Gleason



Source: Harris County Flood Control District | Data analysis and created by Jordan Rubio



Projects favor White Oak Bayou, Cypress Creek, Addicks Reservoir

Aside from the countywide projects, the bond proposal favors White Oak Bayou, Cypress Creek and Halls Bayou, part of Greens Bayou. Jackson Bayou, where chemical explosions rocked the Arkema plant in Crosby, and Carpenters Bayou have the fewest projects under the proposal.

Source: Harris County Flood Control District | Data analysis by Jordan Rubio | Map created by Rachael Gleason



Flood bond money by Harris County precinct

Precinct 1 Commissioner Rodney Ellis would receive the least amount of project money from Harris County but would have the highest total amount with grants compared to other districts, according to the flood control’s list of projects. Precinct 3 Commissioner Steve Radack, who has Addicks and Barker Reservoirs in his district, would receive the most from the county but have the least total amount with grants.
